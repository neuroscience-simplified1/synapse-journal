About Synapse Journal

Synapse Journal is a student-led neuroscience initiative dedicated to making brain science accessible, evidence-based, and engaging for everyone. Our mission is to bridge the gap between scientific research and everyday understanding by translating complex neuroscience into clear, accurate, and compassionate articles.

We believe that knowledge is one of the most effective tools for reducing stigma surrounding neurological conditions. Through educational content, myth-busting articles, and research-inspired writing, Synapse Journal aims to encourage curiosity while promoting empathy for individuals living with neurological disorders.

Our long-term vision is to grow into a trusted student publication that inspires young researchers, science communicators, and lifelong learners to explore the remarkable complexity of the human brain.

Founder

Anvesha Awasthi is a high school student from India with a growing interest in neuroscience, science communication, and medical research. Inspired by witnessing the real-life impact of neurological disorders within her family, she founded Synapse Journal to promote scientific literacy and encourage more thoughtful conversations about brain health.

Through this initiative, she hopes to contribute to neuroscience awareness while continuing to learn, research, and collaborate with the global scientific community.
